02 · Decision 1

Which checkout & pricing path do we run with?

This is the one decision that unlocks or blocks everything else today — GHL setup, automations, and the whole activation plan depend on it.

Option A · Keep it native

Stay on Skool's own checkout

  • $29/mo, already running, 812 real payers — zero migration risk
  • No Klarna, no annual upfront cash, no 50/50 Stripe Connect split
  • Revenue split has to be handled manually/by invoice
  • Fastest path to "just keep operating"
Best if: speed & zero disruption matter more than upfront cash and automatic split right now.
Option B · Migrate to the plan

GHL + Stripe Connect + Klarna, 600€/yr

  • Annual cash upfront per member, automatic 50/50 split
  • Klarna/Affirm/Afterpay unlock ~35% of the buyer base (EU+US+UK)
  • Requires migrating 812 existing members without losing them
  • Needs a clear "grandfather" offer for current $29/mo payers
Best if: the target is Launch #2 scale (300–500 new members, upfront cash) and we accept a short migration window.

⚡ Decision needed from Tudor today

Do we keep the native $29/mo checkout and build the community/content system around it, or do we migrate to the Klarna/GHL annual model for Launch #2 — with a locked grandfather price for the 812 current members? Whatever we pick, it's the first thing that gets built in the next 48–72h.
